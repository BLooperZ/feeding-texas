Despite the economic recovery, 1 in 5 Texas families (18.4%) experienced food insecurity in 2010-2012, according to USDA.

Texas consistently ranks in the top quarter of food-insecure states due to high poverty rates, persistent unemployment in some areas and a general lack of social services.

Food insecurity is most prevalent in households containing children, disabled individuals or the elderly. Every Texas county is touched by the struggle to afford enough food.
